THENKALAI ACHARYA

About Thirumalai Anandalwan

Thirumalai Anandalwan (also known as Ananthalwan or Anantharya) was a great 11th-century Sri Vaishnava Acharya and one of the foremost disciples of Bhagavad Ramanujacharya. He is especially revered in the Thenkalai sampradayam for his complete surrender and dedicated kainkaryam at Tirumala.

Born in Siruputtur near Mysore under Chithirai Nakshatra, he was drawn to the magnetic personality and teachings of Ramanujacharya. When Bhagavad Ramanujacharya called for someone to serve Lord Venkateswara on the difficult Tirumala hills, Ananthalwan courageously volunteered with full prapatti.

Ramanujacharya embraced him and declared him “Aanpillai” (the worthy one). Ananthalwan spent his entire life in selfless service at Tirumala, creating the sacred flower garden and living as a perfect example of Acharya Abhimana and complete dependence on the Acharya.

LIFE OF DEDICATED KAINKARYAM

Life & Service at Tirumala

1
Meeting Bhagavad Ramanujacharya

Drawn by Ramanujacharya’s teachings and compassion, Ananthalwan became his devoted disciple, absorbing the essence of Prapatti and Acharya Nishta.

2
Volunteering for Tirumala Service

When Ramanujacharya sought someone to serve Lord Venkateswara on the wild Tirumala hills, Ananthalwan accepted with complete prapatti. Ramanujacharya embraced him and called him “Aanpillai”.

3
Creating the Sacred Garden (Ananthalwan Thota)

Along with his wife, he cleared the forest and established a beautiful flower garden to offer garlands to Lord Venkateswara, living a life of complete kainkaryam and dependence on the Lord and Acharya.

4
Attaining Moksha

He attained the ultimate goal of eternal kainkaryam at Tirumalai Ananthalwar Thota on the auspicious day of Thiru Adi Pooram, having lived a life of perfect surrender.

LITERARY CONTRIBUTIONS

Literary Works of Ananthalwar

The compositions of this great Thenkalai Acharya reflect deep devotion, Acharya Abhimana, and the glory of Prapatti.

Ramanuja Chatusloki

A profound set of four verses glorifying Bhagavad Ramanujacharya and the places most dear to him, including Tirumala. This work reflects Ananthalwar’s deep Acharya Abhimana and his understanding of the compassionate nature of the Acharya in the Thenkalai tradition.

Key Theme:

The verses highlight how Srirangam, Tirumala, Kanchipuram, and Melkote were especially beloved to Ramanujacharya, showing the intimate connection between the Acharya and the Divya Desams.

Gotha Chatusloki

A deeply moving composition rich in devotion and philosophical depth, glorifying Andal (Goda Devi). This work is especially cherished in the Thenkalai tradition for its tender expression of love and surrender.

Key Theme:

The verses celebrate the glory of Andal and her unique position as the one who “ruled over the Lord” through her pure love and prapatti, reflecting the Thenkalai emphasis on the compassionate and accessible nature of the Divine.

Venkatachala Itihasamala

An important historical and devotional chronicle of Tirumala, comparable to the Koil Olugu of Srirangam. This work preserves the traditional history, practices, and glories of the Tirumala temple as established through the efforts of Ramanujacharya and his disciples.

Significance in Thenkalai Tradition:

It records the reforms and devotional practices instituted at Tirumala, highlighting the central role of Acharya leadership and the importance of regulated kainkaryam in the Thenkalai sampradayam.

MOST BELOVED INCIDENT

The Spot on the Chin

During a severe drought, while digging a well to water the garden plants, Ananthalwan’s pregnant wife was struggling with mud baskets. A young boy (Lord Venkateswara Himself in disguise) insisted on helping her.

In a moment of protective anger, Ananthalwan struck the boy on the chin with a crowbar. The boy disappeared into the temple. The next day, Ananthalwan saw blood on the chin of Lord Venkateswara in the sanctum.

Overcome with remorse and love, he applied camphor to the Lord’s chin and prayed for forgiveness. This incident beautifully reveals the Lord’s saulabhya (easy accessibility) and His willingness to accept even the impulsive actions of His devoted servants as expressions of love.

Camphor on the Chin

To this day, priests apply paccha karpooram to Lord Venkateswara’s chin as prasad, commemorating Ananthalwan’s loving remorse.

The Crowbar

The crowbar is preserved in the temple as a sacred reminder of the Lord’s playful interaction with His devotee.

Bhag Savari Utsavam

This incident is reenacted during the Bhag Savari Utsavam at Tirumala every year.
