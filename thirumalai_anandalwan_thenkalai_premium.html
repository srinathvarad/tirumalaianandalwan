About Anandalwan

Thirumalai Anandalwan (Ananthalwan / Anantharya) was a great 11th-century Thenkalai Sri Vaishnava Acharya and one of the foremost disciples of Bhagavad Ramanujacharya.

Born in 1053 CE under Chithirai Nakshatra in Siruputtur near Mysore, he surrendered at the feet of Ramanujacharya. When his Acharya called for someone to serve Lord Venkateswara on the wild Tirumala Hills, Ananthalwan courageously accepted with complete prapatti. Ramanujacharya embraced him and called him “Aanpillai” — the worthy one.

He lived a life of total dedication, Acharya Abhimana, and selfless kainkaryam at Tirumala, becoming a shining example for all Thenkalai devotees.

Life of Selfless Service at Tirumala

The Courageous Volunteer

When Ramanujacharya asked who would serve the Lord daily on the dangerous Tirumala Hills (then a dense forest), many Acharyas hesitated. Ananthalwan rose and accepted the responsibility with full surrender. Ramanujacharya was deeply pleased and embraced him, declaring him “Aanpillai”.

Creating the Sacred Garden

Ananthalwan and his wife cleared the forest and created a beautiful flower garden (Ananthalwan Thota / Purasai Thota) to offer fresh garlands to Lord Venkateswara every day. He also dug a sacred tank to ensure continuous water supply for the garden and temple use.

The Crowbar Incident

While digging the tank, Ananthalwan’s pregnant wife was struggling with baskets of mud. A young brahmachari (Lord Venkateswara in disguise) offered to help. In protective anger, Ananthalwan struck him with a crowbar on the chin.

The Lord disappeared into the temple. When the priests opened the sanctum, they found blood flowing from the chin of Lord Venkateswara. Overcome with remorse, Ananthalwan applied paccha karpooram and begged for forgiveness. The Lord was pleased with his pure-hearted devotion.

Sri Ananthalwar’s Crowbar
Preserved at Tirumala Temple as a sacred reminder of the Lord’s saulabhya and Ananthalwan’s complete dedication.

The Loving Incident of Purasai Thota

A beautiful devotional legend that reveals the intimate relationship between the Lord and His devotee.

Ananthalwan and his wife lovingly maintained the flower garden for Lord Venkateswara. One night, Lord Srinivasa and Goddess Padmavathi came to enjoy the fragrant flowers. They walked through the garden, playfully trampling some flowers in their joy.

The next morning Ananthalwan was shocked. Thinking someone had deliberately destroyed his kainkaryam, he hid in the bushes the following night. When the divine couple returned, he caught Goddess Padmavathi (mistaking her for a thief) and tied her to a tree, waiting to catch her husband as well.

The next day the priest informed him that the woman he had tied was Goddess Padmavathi Herself. Ananthalwan was overcome with shame. He immediately carried Her in a flower basket and offered Her back to Lord Srinivasa in the sanctum.

Because of this incident, Ananthalwan is revered as the father-in-law (Mamanar) of Lord Venkateswara. This loving episode is joyfully celebrated every year as the Purasai Thota Utsavam at Tirumala.

Literary Works

Ramanuja Chatusloki

Four verses glorifying Bhagavad Ramanujacharya and the Divya Desams dear to him.

अखिलात्मगुणावासमज्ञानतिमिरापहम् । आश्रितानां सुचरणं वन्देऽनन्तार्यदेशिकम् ॥

श्रीमद्रामानुजार्यस्य चरणाम्बुरुहद्वयम् । सदुत्तमाङ्गसन्तार्यमनन्तार्यमहं भजे ॥

Gotha Chatusloki

Four beautiful verses in praise of Andal (Goda Devi), rich in Thenkalai devotional sentiment.

गोदायै नमस्तुभ्यं वेदान्तार्थप्रकाशिनि । यस्याः कृपया लभ्यते भगवद्भक्तिरुत्तमा ॥

Legacy & Continuing Traditions

Ramanuja Shrine at Tirumala

Ananthalwan installed the earliest known idol of Bhagavad Ramanujacharya at Tirumala, given to him personally by his Acharya.

Ananthalwan Satari

The Satari in the Ramanuja shrine at Tirumala is known as the Ananthalwan Satari — a unique and rare honor.

Purasai Thota Utsavam

Every year Lord Venkateswara visits the garden on the day of the incident and honors the sacred tree associated with Ananthalwan.
