Thirumalai Ananthalvan (also known as Ananthazhwan) was one of the greatest disciples of Bhagavad Ramanuja. Born on the same star as Madhurakavi Azhwar (Chithrai-Chithrai), he exemplified complete Acharya nishtai. When no one else dared, he accepted Ramanuja’s command to go to the dangerous Tirumala hills and create a flower garden for Lord Venkateswara’s daily service.

Life Journey

Birth (Chithrai Chithrai)

Born in Sirupuththur (Kiranganur) near Mysore. His thirunakshatram is the same as Madhurakavi Azhwar, reflecting his complete dependence on his Acharya, just as Madhurakavi was on Nammazhwar.

Surrender to Ramanuja

After hearing of Ramanuja’s glories, he surrendered at Srirangam. Ramanuja instructed him to take shelter under Arulala Perumal Emperumanar first, then fully accepted him as a disciple.

The Divine Command – Tirumala

While explaining Nammazhwar’s desire for fresh flower service to Thiruvenkatamudaiyan, Ramanuja asked who would go to Tirumala and create a garden. Ananthalvan immediately volunteered. He created the garden named “Iramanusan” (after Ramanuja) and served fresh flowers daily.

The Crowbar & Divine Forgiveness

While digging a pond for the garden with his pregnant wife, Lord Venkateswara appeared as a young boy to help. Ananthalvan, determined to fulfill his Acharya’s order personally, became angry when the boy helped his wife and chased him with a crowbar. The crowbar struck the boy’s chin; he vanished into the temple.

When the doors opened, blood was seen flowing from the chin of Lord Venkateswara. Ananthalvan fell at His feet in repentance. The Lord forgave him with infinite compassion. To this day, pacchaikarpuram (medicated camphor) is applied daily on the Lord’s chin to cool the wound — a living memorial of this supreme devotion.

Literary Works

Compositions of Thirumalai Ananthalvan

Ramanuja Chatusloki

Four verses glorifying Bhagavad Ramanuja

1
Verse 1
अखिलात्मन्य गुणवासं अज्ञानतिमिरापहम् । आभितानां सुरचरणं वन्दे अनन्तार्य देशिकम् ॥
I bow to the great teacher Anantarya, the abode of all auspicious qualities, remover of the darkness of ignorance, and refuge of all who have surrendered.
2
Verse 2
भूमिं वि विमतिसंस्थितं रक्षः कुलविनाशनम् । शक्तिकूटं विवाद्यं च जय रामानुज देशिकम् ॥
Victory to Ramanuja, who firmly established the truth, destroyed the lineage of the wicked, and is the crest-jewel of all powers.
3
Verse 3
श्रीनिवासस्य प्रणामं तस्य कृपयास्य वशीकृतम् । अङ्कुशं स्वत एव भव्यं जय रामानुज हंसिनम् ॥
Victory to Ramanuja, the great swan, whose prostration to Srinivasa was accepted by grace, and who controls the mind like a goad.
4
Phala Shruti
रामानुज चतुःश्लोकं यः पठेच्छ्रद्धया युतः । प्राप्यते परमं भक्ति वासुदेवस्य शाश्वतीम् ॥
Whoever reads these four verses with faith attains supreme and eternal devotion to Lord Vasudeva.

Goda Chatusloki

Four verses glorifying Andal (Goda Devi)

1
Verse 1
नित्यं भूषा निगम शिरसां निःसम्मोत्तुङ्ग वात्ता कान्तो यस्याः कचविलुलितैः कामुको माल्यरत्नैः । सूक्त्या यस्याः श्रुति सुभगया सुप्रभाता धरित्री सैषा देवी सकल जननी सिञ्चतां मामपाङ्गैः ॥
May Goddess Andal, eternally adorned by the Vedas, shower her gracious glances upon me.
2
Verse 2
माता चेत्तुलसी पिता यदि तव विष्णुचित्तो महान् भ्राता चेत्तु यतिशेखरः प्रियतमः श्रीरङ्गधामा यदि । ज्ञातारः तनयाः त्वदुक्ति सरस स्तन्येन संवर्धिताः गोदा देवी कथं त्वं अन्यसुलभा साधारणा श्रीरसि ॥
If Tulasi is your mother, Vishnuchitta your father, Ramanuja your brother, and Ranganatha your consort — how can you be easily accessible to others, O Goda Devi?
3
Verse 3
कल्पादौ हरिणा स्वयं जनहितं दृष्ट्वैव सर्वात्मनाम् प्रोक्तं स्वस्य च कीर्तनं प्रपदनं स्वस्मै प्रसूनार्पणम् । सर्वेषां प्रकटं विधातुमनिशं श्रीधन्विन्ये पुरे जातां वैदिक विष्णुचित्त तनयां गोदां उदारांस्तुमः ॥
We praise the noble Goda, daughter of Vishnuchitta, born to manifest the principle of self-surrender taught by the Lord at the beginning of creation.
4
Verse 4
आकूतस्य परिष्क्रियां अनुपमां आसेचनं चक्षुषोः आनन्दस्य परम्परां अनुगुणां आरामशैलेशितुः । तद्दोर्मध्य किरीटकोटि घटित स्वोच्छिष्ट कस्तूरिका माल्यामोद समेधितात्म विभवां गोदां उदारांस्तुमः ॥
We praise noble Goda, whose unique glory adorns the chest of the Lord of Venkata hills, enhanced by the fragrance of kasturi and garlands from His crown.

Core Teachings

"A true Srivaishnava is like a crane, like a cock, like salt."

Like a Crane: Ignores the ordinary and waits for a true Gnani to surrender through devotion.
Like a Cock: Picks the good grains (Dravida Veda of Nammalvar) from the chaff.
Like Salt: Completely dissolves himself in Bhagavat, Bhagavata, and Acharya Kainkarya.

Enduring Legacy

The Magizha Tree
Every Adi Pooram and Chithrai Chithrai, Lord Venkateswara visits the garden and honours the tree under which Ananthalvan attained Paramapadam.
Earliest Ramanuja Idol
Installed by Ananthalvan himself at Tirumala (given to him personally by Ramanuja). The Sathari there is called “Ananthalvan”.
Pacchaikarpuram Tradition
Daily application of medicated camphor on the Lord’s chin continues as a living memorial of his devotion.
Madhurakavidasa Lineage
His descendants proudly bear the title “Madhurakavidasa”, reflecting complete Acharya nishtai.
