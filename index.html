The Man Who Chose the Hills

When others hesitated, Ananthalwan courageously accepted his Acharya’s command and dedicated his life to serving Lord Venkateswara at Tirumala through selfless kainkaryam.

Early Life & Surrender to Ramanujacharya

Born in 1053 CE at Siruputtur (near Mysore) in a pious Yajur Veda family, Ananthan (later known as Ananthalwan) mastered the Vedas and shastras from a young age and was immersed in Bhagavata bhakti.

Drawn by the greatness of Bhagavad Ramanujacharya, he surrendered at Srirangam and became his devoted disciple. His intense acharya bhakti earned him the name Ananthalwan.

"He was lost in the ocean of Bhagavad Bhagavata Acharya Bhakti."

The Divine Call to Tirumala

During one of his visits to Tirumala, Bhagavad Ramanujacharya saw the neglected state of the temple and hills. He assembled his 74 disciples and asked who would undertake daily kainkaryam on the dangerous, forested hills.

"Beloved Acharya! Bestow the blessing of serving the Lord of the Seven Hills on this humble self. With your grace, I will be happy to undertake this service."

— Ananthalwan (Anantharya)

Ramanujacharya was overjoyed. He embraced him and declared before everyone: “Anantharya, You are truly the man (Aanpillai).”

Ananthalwan, along with his devoted wife, settled on the hills, cleared the wilderness, created the beautiful flower garden (Ananthalwan Thota), and began daily pushpa kainkaryam — offering fresh fragrant garlands to Lord Venkateswara.

The Garland Leela & Madhurakavi Parallel

One day while making the garland, Ananthalwan got delayed. The Lord sent word asking him to come immediately. He refused, saying he would come only when the garland was finished.

The Lord, in pranaya kopa, told him to leave Tirumala. Ananthalwan fearlessly replied that he was there on his Acharya’s command, not the Lord’s command, and would rather disobey the Lord than his Acharya.

The Lord was greatly pleased and declared that Ananthalwan’s acharya bhakti was equal to that shown by Madhurakavi Azhvar to Nammazhvar. From that day onwards, he and all his descendants are known as Madhurakavi Dāsan.
The Chin Leela & The Sacred Crowbar

While digging the tank with intense zeal, Ananthalwan’s pregnant wife was helping. A young brahmachari offered to assist her. In his single-pointed protectiveness of kainkaryam, Ananthalwan threw the crowbar. It struck the young man, who disappeared into the sanctum sanctorum.

When Ananthalwan entered, he saw blood flowing from the chin of Lord Venkateswara’s murti. Realizing it was the Lord Himself who had come to help, he applied pacchaikarpuram and begged forgiveness. The mark remains visible on the Lord’s chin to this day. The crowbar is preserved at the temple entrance as a sacred memento.

Teachings & Literature

Goda Chatusloki & Ramanuja Chatusloki

Ananthalwan composed beautiful verses on Andal and highlighted the four places dearest to Ramanujacharya: Srirangam, Tirumalai, Kanchipuram, and Melkote.

What is a True Sri Vaishnavite?

"A true Sri Vaishnavite is like a crane, like a cock, like salt."

Crane — Awaits a true Gnani to surrender.
Cock — Picks only the Dravida Veda.
Salt — Dissolves in kainkaryam with humility.

Eternal Legacy

Ramanuja Shrine at Tirumala

Ananthalwan’s final sacred gift. The murti was given by Ramanujacharya himself. The satari used here is named after him.

Festivals & Descendants

Purasai Thota Utsavam • Avatharotsavam in Chithirai • All descendants proudly call themselves Madhurakavi Dāsan.
